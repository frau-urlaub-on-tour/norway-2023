Lofoten Day 2

The day starts with a jump into the lake. Well, more of a timid waddle actually. But I do manage to last for about a minute before the cold becomes too much to bear. We are taking it very slow this morning, enjoy our deck with the lake views and don't get going until about 1 pm. Today we are exploring the Sandoya peninsula where - as we are to learn from Frode - the first settlement of the Lofoten was founded. Another interesting fact we come to learn about is that in this part of the Vestvagoya community you can find the oldest (3 billion years) rocks. After a short drive by car we start our walk around the little peninsula and are rewarded with more stunning views of distant mountains rising up behind the water. And more solitude. Another wet moment, this time in the Atlantic, is a Must. The deserted beach we find ourselves on just invites to try the waters. Bloody cold as well.
